Centralised and Decentralised Exchanges

Cryptocurrency exchanges are platforms where users buy, sell, and trade digital assets. There are two primary types: centralized exchanges (CEXs) and decentralized exchanges (DEXs).

🏛 Centralised Exchanges (CEXs)

CEXs are operated by companies that provide trading infrastructure, manage order books, and hold custody of users’ assets. Examples include Binance, Coinbase, and Kraken.

  • Pros: High liquidity, fast transaction speeds, user-friendly interfaces, fiat support.
  • Cons: Requires trust in the platform, custodial risks, potential for government regulation or censorship.

🌐 Decentralised Exchanges (DEXs)

DEXs operate using smart contracts on blockchains. Users retain control of their private keys and trade peer-to-peer. Popular DEXs include Uniswap, PancakeSwap, and SushiSwap.

  • Pros: Non-custodial, censorship-resistant, greater privacy.
  • Cons: May have lower liquidity, higher fees during congestion, and steeper learning curve.

🤔 Which One Should You Use?

If you're a beginner, CEXs offer a more familiar experience similar to online banking. If you value privacy and control, DEXs offer a more decentralized approach.
