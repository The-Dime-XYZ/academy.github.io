What to Do If Your Wallet Is Compromised

Discovering that your crypto wallet has been compromised can be alarming, but taking swift and informed action can minimize the damage. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to respond if your wallet or associated accounts have been breached:

🚨 1. Act Quickly – Move Your Funds

If you still have access to your wallet, immediately transfer any remaining assets to a secure address—preferably a new wallet with freshly generated keys or a trusted cold wallet. Time is critical to prevent additional losses.

🔍 2. Revoke Suspicious Token Approvals

Use tools like Etherscan Token Approval Checker or Revoke.cash to remove token approvals you've granted to smart contracts. Some exploits rely on having permission to move your tokens.

🔐 3. Secure Your Associated Accounts

Change passwords for your email, crypto exchanges, and social accounts immediately. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible. If you use a password manager, update your master password too.

🔎 4. Identify the Breach

Try to determine how your security was compromised:

  • Did you click on a suspicious link or connect your wallet to an unknown site?
  • Was your device infected with malware?
  • Was your seed phrase stored insecurely or leaked?

Understanding the cause helps you prevent repeat incidents.

📣 5. Report the Incident

Notify the platform or service involved (e.g., an NFT marketplace, DeFi app, or wallet provider) if applicable. Also consider reporting the wallet address to blockchain explorers as compromised.

🛡 6. Educate and Rebuild

Once you've contained the breach, review your security practices:

  • Use hardware wallets for long-term storage
  • Store seed phrases offline and never digitally
  • Regularly audit wallet connections and revoke permissions
  • Avoid clicking on unsolicited DMs or email links

Recovering from a breach can be difficult, but it's a valuable reminder of how critical good security hygiene is in Web3.
