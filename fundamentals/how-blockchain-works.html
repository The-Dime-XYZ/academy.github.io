How Blockchain Works

Blockchain is the foundational technology behind cryptocurrencies and Web3. It is a distributed digital ledger that records transactions across many computers in a secure, transparent, and tamper-resistant way.

🧱 What is a Block?

A block is a collection of data—usually transaction records. Each block contains:

  • A list of transactions
  • A timestamp
  • A reference (hash) to the previous block

Blocks are linked together in chronological order to form a chain—hence, the name "blockchain."

🔗 How Blocks are Added

New blocks are added to the blockchain through a process called consensus, where participants in the network agree on the validity of transactions. Two common methods are:

  • Proof of Work (PoW): Computers solve complex puzzles to validate transactions. This is used in Bitcoin.
  • Proof of Stake (PoS): Validators are chosen based on the amount of cryptocurrency they hold and are willing to “stake.” Used in Ethereum 2.0 and other chains.

🛡 Why It's Secure

Blockchains are considered secure because:

  • Each block contains the hash of the previous block—if one is altered, the chain breaks.
  • The ledger is distributed—no single point of failure or control.
  • Consensus rules prevent invalid transactions.

🌐 Real-World Applications

Besides powering cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum, blockchain is used in:

  • Supply chain tracking
  • Voting systems
  • Digital identity
  • Decentralized finance (DeFi)
