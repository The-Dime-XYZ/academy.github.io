Introduction to DeFi

Decentralized Finance (DeFi) refers to a new financial system built on public blockchains like Ethereum, aiming to eliminate the need for traditional financial intermediaries such as banks and brokers.

🔍 What Makes DeFi Unique?

  • Trustless: Transactions are verified through smart contracts instead of relying on third parties.
  • Open and Inclusive: Anyone with an internet connection and a crypto wallet can participate.
  • Programmable: Developers can build custom logic into financial tools using smart contracts.

📊 Core DeFi Use Cases

  • Lending & Borrowing: Platforms like Aave and Compound let users lend their crypto to earn interest or borrow against it.
  • Decentralized Exchanges (DEXs): Like Uniswap, where users trade directly from their wallets.
  • Stablecoins: Pegged to fiat currencies, these are essential for reducing volatility.
  • Yield Farming: Users move assets between protocols to earn returns.

⚠️ Risks and Considerations

  • Smart Contract Bugs: Code vulnerabilities can be exploited.
  • Volatility: Crypto asset prices can change rapidly.
  • Impermanent Loss: A risk in liquidity pools when token values diverge.

DeFi represents the cutting edge of finance—transparent, permissionless, and decentralized—but it also requires due diligence and caution when participating.
