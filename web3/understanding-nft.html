Understanding NFTs

Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs) are a type of digital asset that represent ownership or proof of authenticity for a unique item or piece of content on a blockchain. Unlike cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin or Ethereum‚Äîwhich are fungible, meaning each unit is the same‚ÄîNFTs are distinct and cannot be exchanged on a one-to-one basis.

  • Metadata: Each NFT carries information that distinguishes it from other tokens. This might include a name, description, and link to digital files like images or videos.
  • Provenance: Blockchain technology allows every NFT‚Äôs creation, ownership, and transaction history to be publicly verified.
  • Scarcity: Creators can issue limited editions or 1-of-1 NFTs, making them rare and collectible.

üé® Popular Use Cases

  • Digital Art: Artists mint works as NFTs to sell and track ownership online.
  • Collectibles: Trading cards, avatars, or in-game items tied to a specific NFT.
  • Gaming: Players own unique weapons, outfits, or characters that can be used or traded across platforms.
  • Music and Media: Musicians and creators sell rights or experiences through NFTs.
  • Event Tickets: NFTs can serve as entry passes with anti-fraud and resale control features.

  • ERC-721: The original standard for NFTs on Ethereum.

  • Marketplaces: OpenSea, Blur, Rarible, Magic Eden, and LooksRare are popular platforms to trade NFTs.
