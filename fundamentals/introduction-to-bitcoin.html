Introduction to Bitcoin

Bitcoin is the first decentralized digital currency, launched in 2009 by an unknown individual or group using the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto. It introduced a revolutionary form of peer-to-peer value exchange that eliminates the need for banks or intermediaries.

üîó Why Bitcoin Matters

Bitcoin was created as a response to the 2008 financial crisis. It offers an alternative to traditional financial systems by giving individuals full control over their money. Its core features include:

  • Decentralization: No central authority controls Bitcoin; it's maintained by a global network of nodes.
  • Limited Supply: Only 21 million bitcoins will ever exist, making it deflationary.
  • Borderless: Can be sent or received anywhere, anytime.
  • Transparent: All transactions are recorded on a public blockchain.

Bitcoin transactions are verified and added to the blockchain by miners using a process called Proof of Work (PoW). This makes the network secure and resistant to fraud.

Users store their Bitcoin in digital wallets, which allow them to send, receive, and manage their assets.

üí° Use Cases

  • Digital store of value (often called "digital gold")
  • Medium of exchange in peer-to-peer payments
  • Tool for financial sovereignty in regions with unstable currencies

As the first successful implementation of blockchain technology, Bitcoin laid the foundation for the entire cryptocurrency ecosystem.
